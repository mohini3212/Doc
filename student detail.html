SCHOOL INFORMATION

The Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan (transl.â€‰Central School Organization) is a system of central government schools in India that are instituted under the aegis of the Ministry of Education, Government of India. As of April 2023, it has a total of 1,253 schools in India, and three abroad in Kathmandu, Moscow and Tehran. It is one of the world's largest chains of schools and also the largest chain of schools in India.

The Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan or 'Central School Organisation'(as previously known), oversees the functioning of the schools with its headquarters in New Delhi.[3] The administration of this body is based on three levels. The chairman of Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan is always the Minister in Charge of the Ministry of Education of the Government of India; the deputy chairman is the Minister of State of the Ministry of Education. The real working power lies with the Commissioner of KVS. There are Additional Commissioners and Joint commissioners to accompany the Commissioner in the administration of KVS in different fields.


  • No tuition fee for boys up to class VIII, girls up to class XII and SC/ST students and children of KVS employees.
  • Quality Education: Known for providing quality education at affordable costs.
  • Education System: KVs follow the CBSE curriculum.
  • Facilities: Typically well-equipped with libraries, labs, sports facilities, etc.
  • Extracurricular Activities: Offer a range of activities including sports, arts, and cultural programs.
  • Admissions: Primarily for children of transferable central government employees, but also open to the public.
